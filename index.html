Citation Grouper

The idea behind the CitePaL is to provide build a tool that aids researchers in quickly accessing relevant background and motivation for a given paper. The main use case is a researcher who encounters a new paper in a line of research that they are less familiar with. Using our tool, the researcher would input a single paper, and receive a list of three or four key papers that the given paper builds on, along with an interactive interface to explore the corpus of reference papers. Here we have a mock up of what this might look like. At the top you can enter the semantic scholar URL to any paper of interest. You will then get a drop down hierarchy of all the paper's references grouped by topic, along with a suggestion for 3-4 most foundational background papers. Below you will see a few summary plots that allow you to explore references from the paper may be most relevant to you.

The papers to be grouped can be either the CSE599D papers (by default), or they can be the references of an article. This works particularly well for browsing references in a review article.
